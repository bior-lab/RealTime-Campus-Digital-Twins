# Site Architecture

## Design Position

The dashboard should not only report energy use. It should explain energy use, forecast demand, identify anomalies, and connect campus behavior to sustainability targets.

## Target Users

- Students and staff who need public-facing energy information.
- Campus sustainability teams who need communication support.
- Facility teams who need building-level signals.
- Researchers who need a structured EMIS case platform.

## Proposed Pages

### 1. Campus Overview

Purpose: provide a summary of current campus energy status.

Core content:

- current electricity demand;
- current cooling demand;
- daily electricity use;
- daily carbon emissions;
- solar generation;
- forecasted peak time;
- AI explanation of the main demand drivers.

### 2. Energy Map

Purpose: show how energy use varies across campus zones and buildings.

Core content:

- map of campus zones;
- building color scale by energy use intensity or current load;
- filters by building type, faculty, zone, and meter status;
- links to building profiles.

### 3. Building Profile

Purpose: provide building-level information.

Core content:

- 24-hour, 7-day, monthly, and annual energy trends;
- electricity, cooling, water, and carbon indicators;
- energy use intensity;
- building area, use type, and operating pattern;
- comparison with buildings of the same type.

### 4. AI Insights

Purpose: explain energy patterns and identify operational signals.

Core content:

- demand forecast;
- anomaly detection;
- baseline deviation;
- possible drivers;
- confidence range;
- suggested actions.

### 5. Carbon and Solar

Purpose: connect energy use to carbon and renewable generation.

Core content:

- Scope 1 and Scope 2 trend;
- solar PV production;
- avoided emissions;
- progress against NUS 2030 targets;
- contribution by campus zone or building group.

### 6. Cooling and Tropical Campus

Purpose: show the role of cooling demand in a tropical campus.

Core content:

- cooling load trend;
- outdoor temperature and humidity;
- thermal comfort indicators;
- cooling demand forecast;
- cooling-related energy-saving opportunities.

### 7. Compare Buildings

Purpose: support comparison across selected buildings.

Core content:

- side-by-side energy use;
- energy use intensity;
- carbon emissions;
- water use;
- operating schedule indicators;
- ranking within the same building category.

### 8. Ways to Save Energy

Purpose: translate dashboard insights into user actions.

Core content:

- actions for students;
- actions for offices;
- actions for laboratories;
- actions for residential buildings;
- actions for events and teaching spaces.

### 9. Data Method

Purpose: document how the dashboard calculates and interprets data.

Core content:

- data sources;
- meter types;
- update frequency;
- data quality rules;
- baseline method;
- carbon factor;
- energy price assumptions.

### 10. Projects and Impact

Purpose: present campus energy and sustainability projects.

Core content:

- energy efficiency projects;
- solar PV projects;
- green buildings;
- cooling strategy;
- measured savings;
- public impact summary.

## First Implementation Priority

The first website version should include:

1. Campus Overview.
2. Energy Map.
3. Building Profile.
4. AI Insights.
5. Data Method.

Other pages can be added after data access and audience requirements are confirmed.
